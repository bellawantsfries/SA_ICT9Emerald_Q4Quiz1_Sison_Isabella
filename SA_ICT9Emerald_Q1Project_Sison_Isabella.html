NOLI ME TANGERE

Juan Crisostomo Ibarra y
Magsalin (Ibarra)
  • The protagonist of the book is Crisostomo Ibarra.
  • He is a young, educated Filipino who has returned from Europe, and he stands in for the optimistic
    and progressive side of the Filipino people. Ibarra is motivated by a desire to make his countrymen's life better.
  • His persona represents the desire for progress in the Philippines under Spanish colonial rule.
    • María Clara de los Santos
    y Alba (Maria Clara)
  • Crisostomo Ibarra's love interest and Capitan Tiago's daughter is Maria Clara.
  • She personifies innocence and purity and represents the hopes for a better future shared by many Filipinos.
  • Maria Clara is a very devout woman, and the novel makes use of her unshakeable commitment to her Catholic faith.
    • Elias
  • Ibarra develops a close acquaintance with Elias, a figure who is cryptic and secretive.
  • He stands in for the downtrodden and underprivileged Filipinos, and his efforts and suffering
    are representative of that of the average person.
  • Elias, who plays a crucial part in the plot of the book, represents tenacity and resistance
    to colonial oppression.
    • Dámaso Verdolagas (Padre Damaso)
  • Padre Damaso, a corrupt and abusive Spanish friar who has enormous influence in the town of San Diego,
    serves as the story's main adversary.
  • He personifies the exploitative and authoritarian traits of the Spanish clergy in the Philippines,
    demonstrating how the Church has a negative impact on Filipino culture.
    • Don Santiago de los Santos (Kapitan Tiago)
  • Capitan Tiago, a wealthy Filipino who hosts social events and parties, is a complicated person.
  • He keeps a strong relationship with the friars while also being sympathetic to the hardship
    of the common people, reflecting the internal difficulties that the Filipino elite during that
    time encountered frequently.
    • Don Rafael Ibarra
  • The father of Crisostomo Ibarra, Don Rafael Ibarra, was a prosperous landowner who had high expectations for the development and modernization of the Philippines.
  • Central to the topics of the book are his legacy and ambition for the nation.
    • Narcisa (Sisa)
  • Sisa is a tragic figure and the mother of Crispin and Basilio, two young boys.
  • Her fall into madness is a moving illustration of the anguish Filipino mothers endured during the time
    of Spanish colonization.
  • The persona of Sisa emphasizes the human cost of colonial subjugation.
    • Isabel Alba (Tia Isabel)
  • Tia Isabel, Maria Clara's aunt, is a devout Christian.
  • She offers Maria Clara spiritual direction, and the book analyzes the difficulties of religious piety
    in colonial times through her character.
    • Don Anastasio (Pilosopo Tasyo)
  • A clever and eccentric philosopher named Pilosopo Tasyo opposes the Spanish colonial system.
  • He acts as Crisostomo Ibarra's tutor while promoting intellectual advancement and social
    transformation in the Philippines.
    • Don Filipo Lino
  • Don Filipo is a diligent educator who is committed to educating the next generation and who
    is outspoken against the abuses committed by the friars.
  • His persona stands in for colonial-era Philippine hopes for social and educational
    transformation.
